# Domain 5 Success Criteria

## Assessment Completion Criteria

Domain 5 is complete when the following outcomes are achieved:

* A normalized inventory exists for users, groups, computers, service accounts, trusts, ACLs, GPOs, delegation settings, privileged groups, and Tier Zero assets.
* Active Directory audit evidence covers forest and domain health, Group Policy, organizational units, DNS, domain controllers, SMB, LDAP, Kerberos, and administrative exposure.
* Identity findings are documented with evidence, risk score, ATT&CK mapping where applicable, owner, remediation, and validation method.
* Attack paths and privilege exposures are assessed using authorized graph analysis and configuration evidence.
* Credential and authentication controls are assessed, including password policy, service-account hygiene, NTLM, LAPS, gMSA, Kerberos configuration, and legacy authentication exposure.
* Approved Purple Team scenarios produce documented telemetry and detection outcomes.
* Splunk and Sigma detection content is mapped to collected or simulated evidence and ATT&CK techniques.
* A prioritized remediation roadmap includes owners, priorities, expected exposure reduction, and retest requirements.
* Technical and executive reports distinguish proposed, simulated, collected, validated, remediated, and retested claims.
* The repository is polished, reproducible, and suitable for portfolio review.

## Executive Success Statement

Lighthouse Technology can demonstrate that identity risk was assessed through defensible evidence, validated safely, translated into prioritized remediation actions, and measured through detection and retest outcomes.
